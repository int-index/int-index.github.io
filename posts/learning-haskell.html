Myth: in order to get started with Haskell, one must first learn category theory, because monads.

Reality: the only background that is truly required is covered by the school curriculum. If you know what an equation is, you will do just fine.

You can master the basics of Haskell in weeks. Below I break it down into a collection of topics, and at some point I will write a tutorial for each. For now, I hope it can at least serve as a roadmap or a checklist:

  1. Lambda calculus.
  2. Algebraic data types.
  3. Pattern matching.
  4. Lazy evaluation.
  5. Parametric polymorphism.
  6. Type classes.
